Un peu d'histoire sur ce sport méconnu

Au XVIe siècle, la boxe thaïlandaise faisait partie de l'entraînement militaire.
Le roi Naresuan le grand (r. 1590-1605) aurait encouragé sa pratique à ce titre.
Il atteignit sa plus grande popularité au début du XVIIIe siècle, sous le règne de Pra Chao Sua, « le Roi Tigre ».
Elle est surnommée l'« art des huit membres » en référence aux huit parties utilisées des bras et des jambes2.
Les pratiquants sont appelés nak muays.

The History of Muay Thai

Muay Thai is a martial art that originated in Thailand. It's known for its use of punches and kicks, as well as knee and elbow strikes. Muay Thai also features grappling techniques such as clinch fighting, throws and takedowns.\ Muay Thai has been around since at least the 18th century, but it wasn't until the 1920s that it became popular outside of Thailand thanks to King Rama VII who promoted the sport throughout his kingdom. Since then, Muay Thai has become one of the most popular forms of combat sports worldwide with millions of practitioners worldwide today!

Muay Thai Techniques

Muay Thai techniques are used in a variety of situations, including: * Stri Techniques - Punching and kicking are the most basic Muay Thai techniques. Punches can be thrown with either hand, but kicks are only performed with the front leg. Kicks can be aimed at the head or body, depending on the angle of attack and range between fighters. * Grappling Techniques - These include throws, sweeps and takedowns that may be used to take down an opponent so that you can get closer for better striking opportunities or apply ground-and-pound from top position (on top). They also include submissions such as armlocks/wristlocks/chokes which are used to force opponents into submission by causing pain or injury rather than simply overpowering them physically like grappling does; these submissions require great skill because they're hard to escape once applied correctly! * Clinch Techniques - This category includes clinching up against the ropes where both fighters try desperately not only avoid being hit also ways around their opponent's defenses while trying their hardest not get hit themselves!"

Training for Muay Thai

Training for Muay Thai is a combination of physical training, mental training and diet. The physical training involves learning the various techniques of Muay Thai such as kicks, punches and elbows. The mental aspect involves learning how to control your emotions during a fight so you can focus on your opponent without being distracted by anything else going on.The diet part of Muay Thai involves eating healthy foods that provide energy while keeping your body fit enough to perform in the ring or during practice sessions at the gym.

Muay Thai Organizations

There are many organizations that govern Muay Thai. The World Muay Thai Council is one of them, and it was founded in Bangkok in 1989. It has members from Thailand, Japan and Australia; its goal is to promote the sport worldwide through events like the World Cup Championship for Amateur Muaythai Athletes.\ The International Federation of Muaythai Amateur (IFMA) was established in 1995 as an umbrella organization nationalations around the globe who wish to promote amateur Muay Thai competitions within their own countries or regions.
